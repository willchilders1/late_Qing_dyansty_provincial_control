Education through Grade Eight

Kenya's primary education system is similar in structure to the American system, with children from 5 to 7 starting at grade one and continuing through grade 8. While significant progress has been made on the school systems in Kenya, different geographical areas see the rate of both gender disparity and general enrollment vastly differ. For intance, the northern and eastern portions of the country, as shown on the map, has far fewer schools with smaller enrollment. Additionally, there are often more boys than girls enrolled at a young age. This disparity is not nearly as severe nearer to the south, which hosts Kenya's capital city Nairobi. Access to civic services in the south is very different than the north, and ways of life differ drastically between these two points.
